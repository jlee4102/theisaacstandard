# Anker Soundcore Q20i — 60-second Shorts script

**Target:** 55-58 seconds spoken (~9:9 vertical, fast cuts, 1-line-per-cut pacing)
**Purpose:** Top-of-funnel — drives subscribers to the long-form review. End with a clear "full review in description" CTA.

---

## 0:00–0:03 — Hook

[VISUAL: Q20i headphones, fast push-in. Big bold text overlay: "DON'T BUY THESE IF..."]

> Three things nobody tells you about the Anker Q20i.

## 0:04–0:14 — Thing 1

[TEXT OVERLAY: "1. CABLE = NO ANC"]
[B-roll: 3.5mm cable plugging in, ANC indicator]

> One. Plug in the three-and-a-half millimeter cable and the noise cancellation turns off completely. So on a flight, when you want to plug into the seat-back screen and keep ANC on for the engine noise — you can't.

## 0:15–0:25 — Thing 2

[TEXT OVERLAY: "2. MIC DIES IN NOISE"]
[B-roll: café environment, phone call]

> Two. The microphone is fine in a quiet room. Take a call from a café, an open office, or anywhere with background noise, and your callers will hear all of it. The mic can't isolate your voice from the room.

## 0:26–0:38 — Thing 3

[TEXT OVERLAY: "3. EQ CAN'T FIX THE TUNING"]
[B-roll: Soundcore app EQ screen, fast scroll through presets]

> Three. The Soundcore app has twenty-two EQ presets and a five-band custom EQ. None of them fix the V-shaped tuning. The mids are scooped, vocals sit behind the kick drum, and you can't software your way out of how the hardware is voiced.

## 0:39–0:48 — The redemption

[TEXT OVERLAY: "BUT..."]
[B-roll: Q20i in use, travel context]

> That said — the ANC is genuinely good at this price. The battery's actually about forty-nine hours, not the claimed sixty, but still excellent. And they're comfortable for long sessions.

## 0:49–0:58 — Verdict + CTA

[TEXT OVERLAY: "3.9 / 5 — FULL REVIEW: theisaacstandard.com"]

> So: yes for commuting, travel, bass-heavy music. No for acoustic listening, video calls, or in-flight wired use. Three-point-nine out of five. Full breakdown — link in bio.

# Production notes

- Vertical 9:16 (1080×1920)
- Fast cuts: every 2-4 seconds (matches modern Shorts/Reels/TikTok pacing)
- Big text overlay for each "Thing" — readers should be able to follow muted
- Keep your voice fast but not rushed — aim for ~155 wpm on Shorts (vs ~150 on long-form)
- Last 3 seconds: hold the rating + URL on screen for screenshots
